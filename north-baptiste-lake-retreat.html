About the Retreat

North Baptiste Lake Retreat is nestled on the shores of beautiful Baptiste Lake, between the charming villages of Bancroft and Maynooth, just south of Algonquin Park, making it the perfect halfway escape between Ottawa and Toronto. Surrounded by natural beauty and local charm, guests can explore Maynooth’s antique shops, visit the Saturday Farmers Market, or enjoy swimming at Papineau Beach and Lake St. Peter Provincial Park. Whether you’re here for lakeside relaxation, outdoor adventure, or cozy nights spent playing games and watching movies, our retreat offers the perfect balance of charm, comfort, and nature.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is North Baptiste Lake Retreat located? +

North Baptiste Lake Retreat is located on Baptiste Lake between Bancroft and Maynooth in Ontario’s cottage country, just south of Algonquin Park.

From Toronto: Hwy 401 East → 115 North → 7 East → 28 North to Bancroft. (Approx. 3 hours)

From Ottawa/Montreal: Hwy 417/17 to Renfrew → 132 → 41 to Denbigh → 28 to Bancroft. (Approx. 2.5 hours from Ottawa)

From Bancroft (20 min): 62 North for 17 km → left on North Baptiste Lake Road for 9.8 km (keep left at the split) to fire sign #1970, across from Sunrise Road.

What activities can guests enjoy at the retreat? +

Swim, canoe, kayak, or paddleboard on Baptiste Lake; visit Papineau Beach or Lake St. Peter Provincial Park for larger sandy beaches. On-site, enjoy projector movie nights, foosball, dart board, yard games, and more. Explore Maynooth’s antique shops or stargaze by the firepit.

How many people can the retreat accommodate? +

Up to 8 adults across three bedrooms (each with a queen bed) and two full bathrooms.

Is North Baptiste Lake Retreat pet-friendly? +

We love animals, but North Baptiste Lake Retreat is a pet-free space to keep the cottage fresh and comfortable for all guests.

What amenities are included with the stay? +

Inside: Wi-Fi, satellite TV, projector TV in games room, and board games. Kitchen essentials plus washer/dryer. Entertainment includes foosball and yard games. Outdoors: deck, firepit, and lake access for swimming and paddling.

How do I book North Baptiste Lake Retreat? +

Is the retreat available year-round? +

Yes—open every season with summer paddling, vibrant autumn colors, cozy winter evenings by the fire, and peaceful spring mornings.
